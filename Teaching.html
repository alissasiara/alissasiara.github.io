I have designed and taught undergraduate and graduate courses on social policy in the Global South at the University of Zurich and the University of Lucerne. The course introduces students to the political economy of social policy in low- and middle-income countries. We examine why countries facing similar development challenges adopt very different approaches to social protection, education, healthcare, and labor markets, and why some programs expand while others stagnate or are rolled back.
